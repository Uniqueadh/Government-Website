About Nepal

About the Federal Democratic Republic of Nepal

Nepal, officially the Federal Democratic Republic of Nepal, is a landlocked country in South Asia. It is mainly situated in the Himalayas, but also includes parts of the Indo-Gangetic Plain. It borders the Tibet Autonomous Region of China to the north, and India to the south, east, and west.

National Identity

Capital: Kathmandu

Official Language: Nepali

Government: Federal Parliamentary Republic

President: Ram Chandra Poudel

Prime Minister: Shusila Karki

Geography and Demographics

Nepal has a diverse geography, including fertile plains, subalpine forested hills, and eight of the world's ten tallest mountains, including Mount Everest, the highest point on Earth. With an estimated population of around 30 million, Nepal is a multiethnic, multilingual, multi-religious and multicultural state.

Government Structure

Nepal is a federal democratic republic divided into seven provinces. The country operates under a multi-party system with executive power exercised by the Prime Minister and the Cabinet, while legislative power is vested in the Parliament.

Our Vision

The Government of Nepal is committed to:

  • Promoting sustainable economic development
  • Ensuring social justice and equality
  • Preserving cultural heritage and natural resources
  • Providing quality public services to all citizens
  • Strengthening democratic institutions and governance
